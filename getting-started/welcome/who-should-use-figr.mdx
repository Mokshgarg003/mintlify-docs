# Who Should Use Figr

Figr transforms how product teams design, but it's particularly powerful for specific types of teams and projects.

<Callout type="info">
**TL;DR:** If you're building digital products with established user bases and need designs that ship quickly, Figr is built for you.
</Callout>

## Perfect Fit Teams

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Product Teams with Existing Apps" icon="mobile">
    **You have:** An established product with user data
    
    **Figr helps:** Create designs that feel native to your existing product, using patterns your users already understand.
    
    **Example:** Adding a new feature to your SaaS dashboard that needs to match existing navigation and feel familiar to current users.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Fast-Moving Startups" icon="rocket">
    **You have:** Limited design resources, need to ship quickly
    
    **Figr helps:** Generate production-ready designs that don't require extensive design review cycles.
    
    **Example:** MVP features that need to look professional but don't have dedicated design time.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Enterprise Teams" icon="building">
    **You have:** Complex design systems, compliance requirements
    
    **Figr helps:** Ensure every design uses approved components and follows established patterns automatically.
    
    **Example:** New admin interfaces that must comply with accessibility standards and use existing design tokens.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Cross-Functional Teams" icon="people-group">
    **You have:** PMs, engineers, and designers collaborating
    
    **Figr helps:** Create a shared workspace where everyone can contribute without design bottlenecks.
    
    **Example:** Sprint planning where PMs can mock up features and engineers can validate feasibility in real-time.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## When Figr Might Not Be Right

<Callout type="warning">
**Honest assessment:** Figr isn't perfect for every situation. Here's when you might want to stick with traditional design tools.
</Callout>

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Brand-New Products" icon="seedling">
    **The challenge:** No existing patterns to learn from
    
    **Consider traditional tools if:** You're creating completely new interaction paradigms or establishing initial design direction.
    
    **Figr becomes valuable:** Once you have some initial product to show and iterate on.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Highly Creative Projects" icon="paintbrush">
    **The challenge:** Need for artistic exploration
    
    **Consider traditional tools if:** You're doing experimental design work, artistic illustration, or brand identity creation.
    
    **Figr shines for:** Systematic interface design and user experience optimization.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Getting Started Assessment

Answer these questions to see if Figr fits your needs:

<Callout type="question">
**Quick Assessment:**

1. Do you have an existing digital product with users?
2. Do you need to maintain design consistency across features?
3. Are design reviews often a bottleneck in your development process?
4. Do you have established design patterns or components?
5. Do multiple team members need to create or modify interface designs?

**3+ Yes answers:** Figr will likely transform your design process
**1-2 Yes answers:** Figr can help, but evaluate your specific use cases
**0 Yes answers:** Consider starting with traditional tools and revisiting Figr later
</Callout>

Product-aware means Figr designs around your specific product, not a generic idea of what an app should look like. Three parts make that possible, and they work together:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Context Pod" href="/product-intelligence/context-pods">
    Your product knowledge graph: recordings, docs, links, specs, and design system.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Design Language" href="/product-intelligence/design-system">
    Components, patterns, typography, spacing, and your product's visual rules.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Infinite Canvas" href="/canvas/canvas-overview">
    Screens, flows, reviews, prototypes, and iterations in one workspace.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Why the combination matters

A Context Pod tells Figr what your product is and what you are building. Your Design Language tells it how the product looks and behaves. The Canvas is where you brainstorm, review and refine. The result is design grounded in your product, not a generic guess.

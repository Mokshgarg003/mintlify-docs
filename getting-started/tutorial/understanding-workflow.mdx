Go straight to screens and you get surface-level design. \
Think through the product first and the design has a reason to exist.

A strong Figr workflow usually looks like this:

1. [Set up product context](/product-intelligence/product-context)
2. [Add product inputs](/inputs/upload-from-computer)
3. [Set up design language](/design-intelligence/design-system)
4. [Ask for a UX review](/product-thinking/review-design)
5. [Map user flows and edge cases](/product-thinking/user-flow)
6. [Create product artifacts](/designing/ideation/draft-prds)
7. [Generate the prototype](/product-thinking/prototype)
8. [Refine with Select & Edit](/designing/edits/click-to-refine)
9. [Export, publish, or hand off](/designing/export/one-click-figma)

Use this workflow especially when working inside an existing product.

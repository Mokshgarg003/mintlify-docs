title: "UX Review"
---

You don't need a meeting or a senior reviewer on call. Point Figr at a screen, flow, or prototype and it returns a prioritized list of UX issues, each pinned to the exact spot and paired with a concrete fix.

## Open a review

Ask Figr to review a screen or flow in chat. When it finishes, it drops a visual feedback card into the conversation. Click **View Review** on that card to open the review.

<Frame caption="Figr posts a visual feedback card in chat. Click View Review to open it.">
  ![03 04 Review Ux (1)](/images/03-04-review-ux-\(1\).png)
</Frame>

The review opens with every issue pinned to the exact spot on the screen, ranked by priority. Click any pin or list item to read the potential issue and the suggested fix.

<Frame caption="Each issue is pinned on the screen, with a potential issue and a suggested fix.">
  ![Figr Design Review panel with prioritized, pinned UX issues](/images/03-04-review-ux.png)
</Frame>

## What Figr flags

- Friction points and places the flow might break
- Unclear hierarchy and confusing copy
- Missing states
- Accessibility risks
- Product logic gaps and patterns that miss user intent

## Example prompts

- "Review this screen for UX friction and prioritize the top issues."
- "Audit this onboarding flow and identify where users may drop off."
- "Review this flow for missing states, unclear hierarchy, and accessibility risks."
- "Compare this screen with the competitor reference and explain the trade-offs."

Review first, redesign later. It is a low-risk entry point for teams skeptical of AI design: you are not asking Figr to build anything, only to review, so you decide what changes.
